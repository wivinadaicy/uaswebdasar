Spring represents renewal and rebirth. The warming weather revitalizes plant life, adding color to the drab outdoors. Dress yourself for the season by adding color and life back into your wardrobe. Start pulling out breezier materials that can keep you cool as the weather warms up.
x
Summer is popular with shorts, dresses and sleeveless clothes. In summer, avoid wearing tight clothes that restrict your circulation. Also, avoid wearing dark fabrics because it can absorb sun rays and trap their heat. It makes you feel even hotter. Colours like white, yellow, beige and other light shades keep you cool.
x
Autumn means it's time to bag up those featherlight linens and break out the heavy cotton and wool shirts. Balancing glamorous trends with the cold reality of fall weather is an unusual challenge. But you can look forward to the comfy, cozy clothes that cool weather brings with this season’s winter-friendly trends that can keep you warm while looking hot.
x
Winter style: the transition from fall to winter is a little less dramatic on the closet than say, summer to fall. A wool coat is the hardest-working piece in your winter wardrobe. It’s the one thing you and everything else will be seeing the most of over the next few months. Typically relegated to cute-outfit cover status, modern silhouettes and bold statement colors give the wool coat it’s time to shine.

Fashion or Costume?

If the industry didn’t get a little weird on occasion, it wouldn’t be fashion. It would be just clothes. Halloween is the one night a year when you can step out in your most outlandish gear without feeling ridiculous. But for many in the fashion flock, such dressing up is a year-round event. Indeed, it’s getting harder to distinguish between the makings of a Halloween costume and the accessory of the moment.

That’s not a bad thing. After all, if the industry didn’t get a little weird on occasion, it wouldn’t be fashion. It would be just clothes. The hotly anticipated Moschino x HnM collection, set to drop Nov. 6, provides plenty of examples of the sweet spot in the middle.

Part raver wear, part French maid feather duster, a pink feathered tube top is plenty versatile when you think of its costume potential.

Need a little head space? Keep close talkers at bay — and the double takes coming in this banana yellow creation.

Part of fashion's art is its artifice. In that way, we all wear costumes every day. Find your own style of costume and be an inspiration to others!

The Season of Sneakers

The youngest member of the Kardashian-Jenner clan has been announced as the latest brand ambassador of sports apparel giant Adidas. The news regarding Jenner’s latest endorsement deal, comes two years after the 21-year-old signed with Adidas competitor Puma for an alleged seven-figure sum. The reality TV star and Kylie Cosmetics founder makes her Adidas debut as the face of the company’s Falcon sneaker.

In its marketing surrounding the revived shoes, Adidas says, “Falcon is for those who do what they want regardless of what anyone thinks. It’s not for the faint-hearted, but for those who know that living unapologetically is the only way to live.” Regarding its decision to sign Kylie, the sportswear heavyweight mentioned, “As a life-long fan of the brand, Kylie embodies the bold spirit of Falcon and we are pleased to announce her as the face of the campaign.”

Paris Fashion Week

The month-long fashion marathon is coming to a close, but not before we have made our way to Paris for the fourth and final leg of the spring/summer 2019 shows. Paris Fashion Week started on a high with Dior's dance-inspired spectacular, and Alessandro Michele put on a show to remember at Gucci – with vocals from Jane Birkin, no less. But it is Hedi Slimane's Celine debut that remains the talk of the town.

On the Parisian pavements, however, it is a more familiar story: prints - leopard, python and even the occasional zebra - continue to reign supreme. So too does the check suit. And where previous fashion weeks have enjoyed warmer weather, the French capital is cooling down, so the layers are starting to pile on. Let the City of Light's finest inspire your style for the season ahead, as seen through the lens of Vogue's street-style photographer Jonathan Daniel Pryce.
